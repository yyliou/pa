Learning Without Labels

Supervised vs. Unsupervised

Chapters 20–21 supervised learning: a known outcome guided the fit. This chapter is the unsupervised side — no labels, only structure to discover:

  • Market basket analysis: which items co-occur in transactions?
  • Clustering: which observations belong together?

Both are exploration tools: they generate hypotheses rather than test them (Hastie et al. 2009).

Market Basket Analysis

The arules package mines association rules of the form \(\{A, B\} \Rightarrow \{C\}\) from transaction data:

library(arules)
data(Groceries)             # built into arules
summary(Groceries)@lengths[1:5]
sizes
   1    2    3    4    5 
2159 1643 1299 1005  855 
rules <- apriori(Groceries,
                 parameter = list(supp = 0.005, conf = 0.5))
inspect(head(sort(rules, by = "lift"), 3))

Reading a Rule

Three numbers qualify every rule:

Note

Support — share of transactions containing all the rule’s items. Confidence — \(P(\text{rhs} \mid \text{lhs})\): how often the right side appears when the left side does. Lift — confidence divided by the right side’s base rate; lift \(> 1\) means genuine association, not popularity.

  • High-confidence, low-lift rules (“⇒ whole milk”) merely restate that milk is popular — lift is the filter that catches this.
  • Thresholds in apriori() trade rule quantity against quality.

k-Means Clustering

Partition \(n\) observations into \(k\) compact groups:

set.seed(5)
km <- kmeans(scale(USArrests), centers = 4, nstart = 25)
table(km$cluster)

13 13 16  8

4  1.4118898  0.8743346 -0.8145211  0.01927104
plot(prcomp(scale(USArrests))$x[, 1:2], col = km$cluster, pch = 19)
  • Scale the variables first; nstart = 25 restarts guard against bad local optima.
  • Choose \(k\) by the elbow of km$tot.withinss across candidate values, or by silhouette width (cluster package).

Hierarchical Clustering

No \(k\) needed in advance — build a tree of merges and cut it later:

d  <- dist(scale(USArrests))
hc <- hclust(d, method = "ward.D2")
plot(hc, cex = 0.55, main = "US states by crime profile")
rect.hclust(hc, k = 4, border = "red")
  • dist() offers euclidean, manhattan, and more; linkage (single, complete, average, ward.D2) changes the tree’s character.
  • cutree(hc, k = 4) extracts cluster labels for downstream use.

Other Unsupervised Tools

  • Model-based clustering: the mclust package fits Gaussian mixtures and selects \(k\) by BIC.
  • Density-based: dbscan finds arbitrarily shaped clusters and flags noise points.
  • Dimension reduction as preprocessing: PCA (Chapter 16) before clustering often improves both speed and quality.
  • Self-organizing maps (kohonen) project high-dimensional data onto a 2-D grid.

Honest Evaluation

Unsupervised results need skepticism most:

  1. Stability — do clusters survive resampling or a different seed?
  2. Silhouette / within-cluster variance — are groups actually compact?
  3. Interpretation — can a domain expert name each cluster?
  4. For rules: validate top rules on a held-out period before acting on them.

A clustering that cannot be named, or a rule that disappears next quarter, is noise wearing a costume.
