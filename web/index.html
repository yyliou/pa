EIB-302-01-A1

Course Overview

This course is a required offering in the English Taught Program in International Business (ETP) at Shih Chien University, taught by Yu-You Liou. It is designed to build a solid working command of the R programming language, from installation and the interactive console all the way to data management, visualization, and applied statistical modeling.

The primary objective of the course is to make students fluent, self-sufficient R programmers. The first half of the semester covers the language itself — syntax, objects, environments, functions, and object-oriented programming — together with the practical skills of importing, cleaning, and reshaping real data. The second half applies that foundation to graphics and statistics: base, lattice, and ggplot2 plotting systems, probability distributions, statistical testing, and regression and classification models.

The required textbook is R in a Nutshell: A Desktop Quick Reference (2nd ed.) by Joseph Adler, published by O’Reilly Media.

Exam Regulations

In alignment with the course’s paperless policy, this examination will be conducted online under the following strict regulations:

  1. Mandatory Attendance Students must take the exam in person. Roll call will be taken five minutes before the exam begins. Late arrival or absence will result in a score of zero.

  2. Network Restriction The exam system tracks IP addresses. You must use the campus Wi-Fi; access from a non-campus IP address will result in a score of zero.

  3. Authorized Resources This is an open-book exam. Any reference materials and AI tools are permitted as auxiliary aids.

  4. Media Prohibition Electronic devices are allowed, but any photography, video/audio recording, or screenshotting will result in a score of zero.

  5. No Communication or Disturbance Interpersonal communication, causing noise or disruptive movements are strictly prohibited. Violators will receive a score of zero.

  6. Copy-Paste Disabled The exam system prohibits copying and pasting. Do not attempt to bypass this feature.
