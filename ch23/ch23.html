Autocorrelation Functions

How strongly does a series correlate with its own past?

acf(turkey.price.ts)
  • Spikes at lag 12 (and 24): annual seasonality — November discounts echo every year.
  • pacf() shows partial autocorrelations — the direct effect of lag \(k\) after removing shorter lags — the standard tool for choosing AR order.
pacf(turkey.price.ts)

Cross-Correlation

Do two series move together, possibly at a delay?

ccf(turkey.price.ts, ham.price.ts, main = "Turkey vs. ham prices")

ccf() correlates one series with lagged copies of another — peaks away from lag 0 suggest one series leads the other.

Decomposition and Smoothing

Split a series into trend + seasonal + remainder:

plot(stl(turkey.price.ts, s.window = "periodic"))
  • stl() uses loess smoothing; decompose() is the moving-average classic.
  • filter() computes moving averages directly; diff() removes trend by differencing — the “I” in ARIMA.

ARIMA Models

The ARMA family models a (differenced) series as autoregression plus moving-average noise:

\[x_t = \phi_1 x_{t-1} + \cdots + \phi_p x_{t-p} + \varepsilon_t + \theta_1 \varepsilon_{t-1} + \cdots + \theta_q \varepsilon_{t-q}\]

fit <- arima(turkey.price.ts, order = c(1, 0, 0),
             seasonal = list(order = c(1, 0, 0), period = 12))
fit

Call:
arima(x = turkey.price.ts, order = c(1, 0, 0), seasonal = list(order = c(1, 
    0, 0), period = 12))

  • order = c(p, d, q): AR order, differencing, MA order; seasonal= adds the seasonal counterpart.
  • Choose orders from ACF/PACF shapes, or by AIC comparison across candidates; tsdiag(fit) checks the residuals.

Forecasting

pr <- predict(fit, n.ahead = 24)
ts.plot(turkey.price.ts, pr$pred,
        pr$pred + 2 * pr$se, pr$pred - 2 * pr$se,
        lty = c(1, 2, 3, 3), col = c("black", "red", "grey", "grey"))
  • predict() returns point forecasts and standard errors; the cone widens — honesty about growing uncertainty.
  • The forecast package automates order selection (auto.arima()) and adds exponential smoothing (ets()).

A Time Series Checklist

  1. Plot the series; note trend, seasonality, breaks.
  2. Stabilize: log-transform if variance grows; difference until stationary.
  3. Inspect acf()/pacf(); propose ARIMA orders.
  4. Fit arima(); compare candidates by AIC.
  5. Validate residuals (tsdiag, Ljung–Box) — they should look like white noise.
  6. Forecast with intervals, never just the point line.
